Terminology

Strikes and Punches
AGE ZUKIRising Punch.
AWASE ZUKI UPunch. Also referred to as MOROTE ZUKI.
CHOKU ZUKIStraight Punch.
CHUDAN ZUKIA punch to the mid-section of the opponent's body.
EMPI UCHIelbow strike (also called HIJI-ATE)
GEDAN ZUKIA punch to the lower section of the opponent's body.
GYAKU ZUKIReverse Punch.
HAISHU UCHIA strike with the back of the hand.
HAITO UCHIRidge-hand Strike.
HARAI TESweeping technique with the arm
HASAMI ZUKIScissor Punch.
HEIKO ZUKI"Parallel Punch" (A double, simultaneous punch).
HIJI ATEMIElbow Strikes.
HIJI-ATEelbow strike (also called EMPI-UCHI)
KAGI ZUKIHook Punch.
KAKUTO UCHIWrist joint strike. Also known as "KO UCHI."
KENTSUI UCHI (Or TETTSUI UCHI)Hammer Fist Stike.
KIZAMI ZUKIJab Punch.
KO UCHIWrist joint strike. Also known as KAKUTO UCHI.
MAE EMPIForward Elbow Strike.
MAWASHI EMPI UCHICircular Elbow Strike. Also referred to as MAWASHI HIJI ATE.
MAWASHI HIJI ATECircular Elbow Strike. Also referred to as MAWASHI EMPI UCHI.
MAWASHI ZUKIRoundhouse Punch.
MOROTE ZUKIU-Punch. Punching with both fists simultaneously. Also referred to as AWASE ZUKI.
OTOSHI EMPI UCHIAn elbow strike by dropping the elbow. Also referred to as Otoshi Hiji Ate.
TATE EMPIUpward Elbow Strike.
TATE URAKEN UCHIVertical back-fist attack.
TATE ZUKIVertical Punch. A fist punch with the palm along a verticalplane.
TEISHO UCHIPalm Heel Strike.
TETTSUI UCHIHammer Strike. Also called KENTSUI.
YAMA ZUKIMountain Punch. A wide U-shaped dual punch.
URA ZUKIAn upper cut punch used at close range. URAKEN Back Knuckle.
