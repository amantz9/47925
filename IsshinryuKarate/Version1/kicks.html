Terminology

Kicks (Geri) and Foot Techniques
ASHI BARAI Foot Sweep
ASHI WAZAName given to all leg and foot techniques.
FUMIKOMIStomp kick, usually applied to the knee, shin, or instep of an opponent.
GYAKU MAWASHI GERIReverse Round-house Kick.
KEAGESnap Kick. (Literally, Kick upward).
KEKOMIThrust Kick ( Literally, Kick Into/Straight ).
MAE GERI KEAGEFront Snap Kick. Also referred to as MAE KEAGE.
MAE GERI KEKOMIFFront Thrust Kick. Also referred to as MAE KEKOMI.
MAWASHI GERIRoundhouse Kick.
MIKAZUKI GERICrescent Kick.
TOBI GERIJump Kick.
UCHI MAWASHI GERIInside Roundhouse Kick.
USHIRO GERIBack Kick.
YOKO GERI KEAGESide Snap Kick. Also referred to as YOKO KEAGE.
YOKO GERI KEKOMISide Thrust Kick. Also referred to as YOKO KEKOMI.
YOKO TOBI GERIFlying Side Kick.

Weapons

BOStaff. A long stick used as a weapon (approximately 6 feet long).
EKKUA Wooden oar used by the Okinawan's which was improvised as a weapon.
JOWooden staff about 4'-5' in length. The JO originated as a walking stick.
KAMASickle
NUNCHAKUTwo 12 inch batons connected at the ends with a short cord.
SAIAn Okinawan weapon that is shaped like the Greek letter 'Psi' with the middle being much longer.
TONFAA farm tool (believed to have been used as a handle for a millstone) developed into a weapon by the Okinawan's.
